## Assessment

**Positives:**
- No accidents
- Oil changes documented through 95k at regular intervals
- The 1.8T Gen 3 is a reasonably reliable engine
- Conventional 6-speed auto (NOT DSG)
- Base S trim = fewer electronics to break

**Concerns:**
- Repo'd at 49k — Owner 1 drove it 36k mi/yr and defaulted. Probably not babied.
- 49k-mile service gap (108k to 157k, 2021–2025)
- VW dealer "engine checked" at 96k — no explanation. The fact Owner 2 went to VW specifically for an engine check (after years at quick-lube places) suggests something was wrong.
- 170k on a 1.8T turbo four — higher mileage territory for this engine
- No brake, suspension, or fluid service documented ever (beyond oil changes)

**The Dealer:** Pristine Motors with 2 five-star and 1 one-star reviews (sold a lemon). 3 reviews total is too small to judge. They're a small volume flip shop.

## Verdict

Fine but unremarkable. At $4,800 it competes directly with the Schererville 328i, and the BMW wins on every axis: better documentation, better engine reputation, zero accidents vs. zero accidents, BMW dealer service vs. quick-lube oil changes. **Skip.**
