# General Buying Advice

## Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI)

**Non-negotiable on any of these cars.** $100–$150 at an independent specialist (BMW shop for the BMWs, etc.). Specifically request:

- Compression/leak-down test if oil consumption is suspected
- Scan for stored and pending fault codes
- Visual inspection underneath for rust, leaks, and structural damage
- Transmission behavior assessment

## Test Drive Checklist (Universal)

1. **Cold start** — listen for rattles, ticking, rough idle in the first 10–30 seconds
2. **Idle** — watch for fluctuating RPM, unusual vibration
3. **0–30 mph acceleration** — feel for transmission shudder, hesitation, or jerking
4. **Highway speed (40–60 mph)** — light throttle, feel for torque converter shudder
5. **Parking lot turns** — full lock both directions, listen for grinding/clunking (AWD systems)
6. **Braking** — any pulsation, pulling, or noise
7. **Fluid checks:**
   - Oil dipstick — correct level, color, any sludge
   - Coolant reservoir — correct level, no oil film
   - Under oil cap — milky residue = head gasket failure
   - Exhaust — sweet smell = coolant leak

## Negotiation Framework

- Know the CARFAX/KBB fair value before you call
- Use documented deferred maintenance as leverage ("water pump hasn't been done — that's $800 I need to budget")
- At this price range, **cash talks** — lead with "I'll pay $X,XXX cash today"
- Small dealers and private sellers are more negotiable than franchise dealers
- If the clutch is original on a 190k-mile manual, that's $1k leverage

## Rust Belt Survival Priorities

All of these cars have spent years in salt-belt states (Michigan, Ohio, Indiana, Illinois, New York). Priority inspection areas:

1. **Frame/subframe** — structural rust is a safety issue and an instant walk-away
2. **Brake lines** — corroded steel brake lines can rupture
3. **Rocker panels** — cosmetic but indicates overall rust severity
4. **Rear wheel arches** — common rust-through point
5. **Exhaust system** — if not already replaced

## Common Red Flag Patterns

| Pattern | What It Means |
|---------|---------------|
| Multiple owners in first few years | Underlying defect being passed around ("hot potato") |
| Repo followed by quick resale | Neglected end-of-ownership + motivated seller |
| Auction purchase followed by immediate listing | Flipper behavior — no knowledge of car's real condition |
| Service gap of 50k+ miles | Either unreported maintenance or genuine neglect |
| Repeated computer/powertrain checks at dealer | Unresolved issue the dealer can't fix |
| "Needs nothing" with warning lights on | Seller is either lying or ignorant |

## OBD2 Scanner Tip

For any car you're seriously considering, bring a **Bluetooth OBD2 scanner** ($20 on Amazon) paired with a free app (like Torque on Android or OBD Fusion on iOS). Plug it in and read stored/pending fault codes. This takes 2 minutes and can reveal issues the seller isn't disclosing. Particularly useful for:

- BMW "Check Control" warnings
- Emissions-related codes
- Transmission fault codes
- Engine misfire counts
