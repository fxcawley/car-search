## Why This One Is Worth Pursuing

The 5-speed manual eliminates the single most expensive failure mode on high-mileage cars — the automatic transmission. This is a fundamentally different proposition than everything else in the search.

- **FB20 engine** — no turbo, simple, reliable
- **5-speed manual** — virtually indestructible; clutch is the only wear item (~$800–$1,200)
- **Symmetrical AWD** — mechanical, not electronic; simpler than BMW/Volvo systems
- **Hatchback** — practical

## Known Failure Modes: 2015 Subaru Impreza (FB20)

| Issue | Severity | Cost | Notes |
|-------|----------|------|-------|
| Head gasket | Red | $1,500–$2,500 | FB20 is much better than old EJ engines but not immune. External oil weeping is livable; coolant breach is serious. |
| Oil consumption | Yellow | Monitoring | Some FB20s consume 1 qt / 3,000 mi. Subaru considers this "normal." |
| Clutch wear | Yellow | $800–$1,200 | At 190k on a manual, may be original — near end of life. |
| Wheel bearings | Yellow | $300–$500/ea | Very common Subaru issue, especially rear. Humming that changes with speed. |
| CV axle boots / joints | Yellow | $200–$400/ea | AWD = 4 CV axles. Clicking in turns = outer joint. |
| Rust | Yellow | Varies | Subarus rust, especially rear wheel arches and brake/fuel lines. |
| Valve spring recall | Red | Free if open | Some 2012–2015 FB20s. Check VIN at subaru.com/recalls. |
| AC compressor | Yellow | $500–$800 | Known weak point. |

## What to Ask on the Phone

**Ownership / history:**
- "How long have you owned it? Are you the original owner?"
- "Why are you selling?"
- "Do you have service records or receipts?"
- "Has it ever been in an accident?"

**Mechanical — deal-breakers:**
- "Has the clutch ever been replaced? Does it slip in high gear under load?"
- "Any oil consumption between changes? How much?"
- "Has the head gasket ever been done? Any coolant loss or overheating?"
- "Any check engine light on? Recently cleared?"

**Mechanical — wear items:**
- "Any humming or droning at highway speed?" (wheel bearings)
- "Any clicking when turning?" (CV joints)
- "When were spark plugs last done?"

**Condition / rust:**
- "How are rocker panels, wheel arches, and subframe? Any rust-through?"
- "Are the brake lines original?"

## Go / No-Go Criteria

**Buy if:**
- No head gasket issues (no coolant loss, no milky oil, no overheating history)
- Clutch engages cleanly (even if replacement needed soon — known cost)
- No frame/subframe rust-through
- No grinding/whining from transmission or differential
- Owner seems straightforward
- Check engine light is off

**Walk away if:**
- Sweet smell from exhaust or bubbles in coolant reservoir (head gasket)
- Milky residue on oil cap
- Clutch slips AND owner claims it's "fine" (dishonesty signal)
- Structural rust on subframe or brake lines
- Transmission grinds going into any gear (synchros — expensive on manual Subaru)
- He won't let you take it to a mechanic
