## N63 Engine -- The Worst BMW Engine in Modern History

The N63 is BMW's "hot-V" twin-turbo V8. The turbos sit between the cylinder banks (in the valley), directly above the oil pan. This design cooks everything around it. Known catastrophic issues:

| Issue | Status on This Car | Typical Cost |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|
| Complete engine failure | Already happened once (replaced at 42k) | $8,000--$15,000 |
| Fuel injector failure | Replaced TWICE (17k and 110k) | $1,600--$2,400 (8 injectors) |
| Spark plug fouling | Replaced THREE times (17k, 107k, 119k) | $400--$600 |
| Valve stem seal failure | Unknown -- causes massive oil consumption | $3,000--$5,000 |
| Turbo oil line leaks | Unknown | $1,500--$3,000 |
| Coolant loss / hose failure | Lower hose replaced (101k), upper hose replaced (107k), coolant flush x2 | $300--$800 per event |
| Timing chain guide wear | Unknown | $3,000--$5,000 |
| Battery drain | Battery replaced twice (17k, 128k) | $300--$500 |
| Aux fan failure | Fan switch replaced (101k) | $500--$1,000 |

BMW acknowledged the N63's problems by issuing a "Customer Care Package" -- essentially an unofficial extended warranty. That's how bad it is.

## Verdict

**Hard pass.** This car is the textbook example of "nothing is more expensive than a cheap luxury car." The N63 is BMW's worst modern engine -- it already destroyed itself once at 42k miles. The replacement engine has the same fundamental design flaws and has already needed injectors and plugs replaced multiple times. The moderate-to-severe all-body damage at every panel location is the same catastrophic pattern as the San Diego 335i.

At $5,500 this is MORE expensive than the Schererville 328i ($4,800), the Cincinnati Impreza ($4,900), or the Cincinnati 335i ($5,000) -- all of which are dramatically safer bets. The 750Li's luxury and 400 hp are seductive, but the N63 will eat your budget alive. **Do not buy this car.**
