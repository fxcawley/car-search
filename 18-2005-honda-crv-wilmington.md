## Concerns

- **$5,700 for a 20-year-old FWD SUV with 196k** -- overpriced by $1,000--$1,500
- **143,000 miles of near-zero documented maintenance** (Owner 3)
- **FWD only** -- no AWD for Michigan winters
- **Trans fluid last changed at 45k** -- 151,000 miles ago. At this point, changing it could actually cause problems (the "don't change old trans fluid" dilemma).
- **No valve adjustments documented** -- tight valves can burn and cause misfires
- **9 years in Columbus with zero service** -- even at low mileage, rubber, seals, and fluids degrade
- **Takata passenger airbag recall** -- status unclear
- **20 years old** -- age kills rubber regardless of mileage (hoses, seals, bushings, brake lines, CV boots)

## What to Ask

- "Has the transmission been serviced recently?" (Critical -- if not since 45k, it's a gamble)
- "Any valve adjustment done?" (Honda-specific concern)
- "Is the passenger airbag recall completed?"
- "Any rust underneath?" (8 years in Columbus)
- "Can I hear a cold start?" (Listen for valve tick -- tight/loose valves)
- "How does the transmission shift when cold?" (Harsh shifts = worn fluid/solenoids)

## Verdict

The K24 Honda engine is legendary, and Florida provenance means it probably escaped the worst of rust. But **Owner 3 treated this car like a disposable appliance for 17 years**, and $5,700 is a premium price that the service history doesn't justify. You're paying the Honda tax.

At $4,000--$4,500 this would be a reasonable gamble on Honda reliability. At $5,700 it's overpriced when the Cincinnati Impreza ($4,900) has a 50k-mile engine, all-dealer service, AWD, and a manual trans -- or the Schererville 328i ($4,800) has zero accidents and all-dealer service. **Ranked #8 -- skip unless they come down to $4,000.**
