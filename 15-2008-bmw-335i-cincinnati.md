## What to Ask

- "Any turbo noise, whistle, or rattle on cold start?"
- "Any hesitation or long-crank starts?" (HPFP symptom)
- "Any rough idle or misfires?" (injector symptom)
- "Any smoke on startup — white or blue?" (turbo seal or injector leak)
- "When were the spark plugs last done?"
- "Any oil leaks currently?" (oil pan gasket)
- "Can I get a cold start and hear the engine?"

## Verdict

**The first 335i worth considering.** Two responsible owners, BMW dealer service, and a massive suspension/drivetrain rebuild. More money invested in maintenance than many BMWs see in a lifetime. However, the N54-specific failure modes (turbos, HPFP, injectors) are not documented as addressed. At 130k, those are the items that could fail.

The choice vs. the 328i comes down to **risk tolerance:**
- **328i = safe choice** — lower repair ceiling, no turbo complexity
- **335i = exciting choice** — more power, better maintained, fewer miles, but if a turbo goes it's $3k+

If you can budget $2k–$3k for potential turbo/HPFP work in the first year, the 335i is arguably the better car.
