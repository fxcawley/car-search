## Assessment

**Positives:**
- 10-year single owner (#2) — stability
- First 46k miles meticulously serviced at VW dealer
- FWD = simpler than AWD (no transfer case, no AWD complexity)
- Lower mileage than the Passat (130k vs 170k)
- Oil pressure sender replacement suggests someone is paying attention

**Concerns:**
- Two service gaps: 46k to 97k (4 years) and 99k to 125k (2 years). About 77k miles with only 2 Jiffy Lube visits.
- No brake, coolant, trans fluid, or spark plug service documented in 130k miles
- Minor accident — right front. Probably cosmetic but no repair record.
- Computer reprograms (transmission at 10k, engine at 40k) — possibly early TSBs/fixes
- Went through auction before landing at Pristine Motors

## Jetta vs. The Field

The Jetta is perfectly adequate, but at $4,600 it's in the same price tier as the Schererville 328i ($4,800), which has:
- All-BMW-dealer service for its entire 148k-mile life
- Zero accidents
- The bulletproof N52 engine

The 1.8T EA888 Gen 3 is fine but it's a turbo four — timing chain tensioner, carbon buildup, water pump are all higher-probability failure modes than anything on the N52. FWD is simpler than xDrive, which is a genuine point in the Jetta's favor, but not enough to overcome the service history gap.

## Verdict

A perfectly adequate car and the best of the two VWs. FWD simplicity is a real advantage over xDrive for maintenance costs. But at $4,600 it doesn't dethrone the Schererville 328i. **Decent #4 option** if you want something simpler and are willing to accept the service gap tradeoff.
