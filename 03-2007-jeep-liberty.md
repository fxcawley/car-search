## Known Issues: 2007 Jeep Liberty (KJ Generation)

- **3.7L V6:** Generally durable but known for valve seat dropping at high mileage (rare but catastrophic), exhaust manifold cracking, oil consumption
- **42RLE Transmission (auto):** Solenoid pack failures, torque converter shudder — the #1 killer
- **Ball joints / lower control arms:** Wear out frequently, especially in Michigan salt/rust
- **Window regulators:** Already replaced one — the other side will likely go
- **Rust:** Michigan car for 19 years — check frame, subframe, rocker panels, rear wheel wells
- **Ignition/electrical:** Ignition switch + starter already replaced
- **Exhaust:** Already fully replaced

**Concerns:**

- 19 Michigan winters = rust is the #1 thing to check
- No transmission fluid service documented ever

**Watch For:**
- Frame/subframe rust (safety critical)
- Transmission behavior (test drive at highway speed, check for shudder)
- Coolant mixing with oil (head gasket)

## Verdict

A much better buy than the Terrain. At $3,300 for a no-accident, single-long-term-owner vehicle with fresh brakes, exhaust, and starter — fair price. The Liberty isn't fancy, but they're simple trucks. **Before buying: crawl under it and look at the frame. If the frame is solid, it's a reasonable $3.3k vehicle. If you see scale rust or holes, walk.**
