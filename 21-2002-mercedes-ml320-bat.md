## M112 Engine -- The Good News

The M112 3.2L V6 is one of Mercedes' most reliable engines from this era. It is **NOT** the M272 with the balance shaft nightmare. Key differences:

| Factor | M112 (this car) | M272 (ML350 / C280) |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Balance shaft issue | No | Yes |
| Design era | Late 1990s, proven | Mid-2000s, problematic |
| Complexity | SOHC, 3 valves/cyl, simple | DOHC, 4 valves/cyl, complex |
| Typical lifespan | 250,000+ mi if maintained | 150,000--200,000 mi |
| Parts cost | Cheap (mass-produced) | Moderate |

The M112 is mechanically simpler and more proven than every other engine in this search (N52, N54, N63, M272, FB20, EA888, K24). It's not exciting, but it's tough.

## The "Sitting Car" Problem

Owner 2 drove 722 miles per year for 12 years. Cars that sit develop their own set of problems:

- **Dried seals and gaskets** -- rubber seals shrink when not lubricated by running fluids
- **Stale fluids** -- brake fluid absorbs moisture (hygroscopic), trans fluid oxidizes, coolant degrades
- **Cracked rubber hoses** -- heat cycling without flow degrades rubber
- **Flat-spotted tires** -- already addressed (new tires by dealer)
- **Battery drain** -- already addressed (new battery by dealer)
- **Fuel system varnish** -- fuel filter replaced by dealer, but injectors may need cleaning

## Verdict

**At $2,900, this is worth bidding on.** The M112 is the most reliable engine in the search, the California provenance eliminates rust (your biggest enemy in Michigan), and 107k miles is low. The service history is atrocious -- but the entry price is so low that you can budget $1,500--$2,000 for catch-up maintenance and still be under $6,000 all-in including shipping.

The risk is real: this is a 24-year-old car that sat for a decade. Dried seals, cracked hoses, and mystery electrical gremlins are all possible. But unlike the N63 750Li or the N54 335i, the M112's failure modes are cheap to fix. A crankshaft sensor is $50. A window regulator is $150. An expansion tank is $30. This is not a car that will hit you with a $5,000 repair bill.

**Bidding strategy:**
- **Sweet spot: $2,500--$3,000** -- best value in the search at this price
- **Still worth it: $3,000--$3,500** -- competitive with top picks after shipping + maintenance
- **Walk away above $4,000** -- at that point the Schererville 328i is a better buy

**First thing to do if you win:** Full fluid change (trans, diffs, transfer case, brake, coolant), expansion tank replacement, and a thorough inspection of all rubber hoses and wiring. Budget $1,500 and a weekend at an independent Mercedes shop.

**Ranked #5.** Could move higher once inspected -- the M112 is genuinely tougher than anything else in the search.
