## The M272 Engine — The Key Issue

### Balance Shaft Sprocket Wear (M272 "Idler Gear" Issue)

- Early M272 engines (2005–2008) had a defective balance shaft sprocket made of softer metal
- Causes timing chain skip, check engine light, eventually catastrophic engine failure
- **Repair cost: $2,000–$4,000** (requires front-of-engine disassembly)
- Mercedes issued a service campaign but not a recall — many cars were never fixed
- At 89k miles, if it hasn't been addressed, it may be fine... or a ticking bomb
- **This is the single most important question to answer before buying**

## The "Infrequently Driven" Factor

89k on 18 years = ~4,900 mi/yr. Consistent with the MIL story. But low-use cars have their own problems:

- **Rubber degradation:** Hoses, seals, bushings, tires deteriorate with age, not mileage. 18-year-old rubber is 18-year-old rubber.
- **Brake corrosion:** Rotors rust from sitting. He mentions "new tires from 2023" but no mention of brakes.
- **Coolant / fluid age:** Could be ancient if changed by mileage rather than time.
- **Battery:** Older Mercedes are notoriously sensitive to voltage drops. If it's been sitting, battery and alternator need checking.
