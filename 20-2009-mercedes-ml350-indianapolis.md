## What Makes This Car Unique

**Nearly every service record in 17 years is at Mercedes-Benz of Indianapolis.** This is the single best dealer-service provenance in the entire search. The dealer knows this car inside and out -- and they're the ones selling it. That cuts both ways:

- **Positive:** They wouldn't put their name on a car they know has hidden problems. They've literally maintained it for 17 years.
- **Neutral:** They also know exactly what it needs and may be pricing accordingly.

## M272 Engine Assessment

The M272 E35 3.5L V6 is the same engine family as the C280's M272 E30. Known issues:

| Issue | Risk Level for 2009 | Notes |
|-------|---------------------|-------|
| Balance shaft sprocket wear | Lower than 2006-2008 (revised design for 2009) | Still same engine family. Listen for rattle at cold start. |
| Intake manifold swirl flaps | Yellow | Can break and get ingested into cylinders. Catastrophic if it happens. |
| Camshaft adjuster solenoids | Yellow | Can cause rough idle, check engine light |
| Oil leaks | Done -- valve cover gasket replaced at 40k | May need again at 160k |
| 7G-Tronic transmission | Generally reliable | Trans was R&R'd at 40k (likely for leak repair, not rebuild) |

The 2009 model year is less risky than the 2007 C280 for the balance shaft issue specifically, but it's the same fundamental engine.

## Verdict

This is a genuinely interesting car. The 17-year all-Mercedes-dealer service history is extraordinary -- you can trace every oil change, every brake job, every repair. The 2009 M272 has lower balance shaft risk than the 2007 model. AWD, V6, SUV practicality. Recent comprehensive maintenance (brakes, cooling system, belt tensioners, tires) means the immediate maintenance burden is low.

The downsides: 16 years old, ~160k+ miles, one accident, A/C compressor replaced twice, recurring power steering issues, and Mercedes parts cost. The unknown price is the wildcard -- if MB of Indy wants $6,000+ it's out of budget. If they're asking $4,500--$5,500, the service history makes it worth a look.

**Ranked #5.** Better documentation than anything below it, but age, M272 risk, and luxury maintenance costs keep it behind the top tier. Call for price first -- that determines whether this is worth pursuing.
